Think for yourself, out loud

I won't tell you what to think — that's the whole point. I ask questions so you can hear your own mind, sort what's genuinely yours from what you absorbed, and move toward the person you actually want to be. I'm not a therapist, and I don't advise or diagnose.

Lenses

A lens is a way of looking — not a set of beliefs to swallow. Each of these traditions spent centuries asking how to live well. You don't have to join any of them. You try one on, see what it reveals about your situation, keep what's useful, and discard the rest. That act of testing rather than adopting is itself the skill: thinking for yourself.

How to use them well

Try a lens on — don't swallow it. The goal is never to become "a Stoic" or "an Existentialist" (that's just a fancier crowd to follow). The goal is to borrow a perspective deliberately, notice where it fits and where it doesn't, and end up with a view that's genuinely yours. Open the Companion and tap a lens to apply it to whatever you're thinking about — then tap another to compare. Prefer a hint? Flip on Suggestions in the Companion's lens bar and it'll occasionally point out a fitting lens — still opt-in; nothing applies unless you tap it.

About & Safety

What this is for

Two ideas hold this whole thing together: become the best version of yourself, and to do that, think for yourself. Most of what runs through our heads in a day is borrowed — opinions from feeds, fears from comparison, "shoulds" from a crowd we never chose. Constellation exists to help you separate what's genuinely yours from what you just absorbed, so the life you build is authored by you and not by an algorithm.

It's a personal reflection tool. You place your thoughts, feelings, and behaviors as points, connect the ones that feel related, and step back to see the shape of your week. The Origin lens shows how much of your inner life you actually authored. The companion is a gentle listener that asks open questions — it never answers them for you, because handing you someone else's conclusions would defeat the entire purpose.

If you are in crisis

This app is not the right tool in an emergency, and it cannot help if you are in danger. If you are thinking about harming yourself or you feel unsafe, please reach out to a real person now:

• Emergency: 911 (US) · 000 (Australia) · 112 (Europe) · or your local number
• US: Call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline)
• Australia: Lifeline 13 11 14
• UK & ROI: Samaritans 116 123
• Anywhere: find a local line at findahelpline.com

What this is not

Constellation does not diagnose, treat, or give medical, psychological, or therapeutic advice. It is not a substitute for a qualified professional. Nothing it shows you is an assessment of your mental health. If something is weighing on you, a doctor, counsellor, or psychologist is the right place to take it.

Your privacy

Nothing you write here is sent to any server — there is no account, no upload, no analytics. Your maps are saved in your own browser on this device (local storage), so they're waiting for you next time. Your companion conversations are never saved at all: they exist only while the tab is open and disappear when you close it. Clearing your browser data, or using a different browser/computer, means your maps start fresh — use Export to keep a backup file you control.

How to use the map

• Add a node for a thought (◇), emotion (◯), or behavior (△).
• Drag nodes to arrange them.
• Connect two nodes you suspect are related.
• Origin lens highlights what you absorbed from outside ("borrowed") versus what's genuinely yours — a small antidote to comparison and groupthink.
• Lenses let you view a thought through a philosophical tradition (Stoic, Existentialist, and more) — as tools to test against your own life, never as beliefs to adopt.
• The point isn't to journal. It's to place things and see how they link.

Honest note

The companion uses simple pattern-matching, not artificial intelligence — it reflects and asks rather than understands. It will sometimes miss the mark. Treat it as a mirror to think against, nothing more.
